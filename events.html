Continnum

  • Analogue signals are those in which information is represented by a continuously variable physical quantity such as spatial position, voltage etc. The event encapsulates the use of transducers to convert physical phenomenon into equivalent electrical signals. 'Continuum' focuses on design of circuits that are useful to derive meaning out of these continuously varying quantities. The event incorporates both actual realization of circuits and simulation while the latter shall aid the participants in case the circuits are voluminous.

Muse

  • "If you have an apple and I have an apple and we exchange these apples then you and I will still each have one apple. But if you have an idea and I have an idea and we exchange these ideas, then each of us will have two ideas."
    -George Bernard Shaw 

    MUSE, the paper presentation provides an ideal platform for the thinkers of this generation. For those who wish to voice their innovative ideas in an effective manner, this event will provide them an opportunity to network, compete and grow with the best.

  • Participating teams will submit an abstract of their paper (adhering to event rules) and present the paper to the judge panel.

  • Phase 1:
    Participants are expected to submit an abstract of their paper strictly in the IEEE format consisting of not more than 2 pages.

    Note:
      a. Last date of abstract submission is 25/3/2015.
      b. Based on the peer review of the abstract, some selected papers will be shortlisted for the final presentation.

    Phase 2:
    During the main event, the contestants will get time to present their paper.

    Note:
      a. The selected participants should submit a hard copy of the paper with abstract in the beginning in A4 sheets not exceeding ten pages (including references, tables and figures).
      b. The oral presentation should be in the form of slides containing brief of your paper.
      c. The presentation should last a maximum of 7 minutes. Following the presentation, there will be a 3 minutes query and answer session by the judges as well as by the other participants.

    • 1. Abstract should be strictly in IEEE format.
    • 2. Maximum 3 team members are allowed.
    • 3. Preference will be given to original papers.

Ignite

  • IGNITE is the business plan contest conducted as a part of UDYAM the techno-management festival of Electronics Engineering Department, IIT(BHU), Varanasi. It seeks to identify budding entrepreneurs from around the country to contribute to India’s buzzing economy.
    We believe new business ideas are the fuel for growth of any economy, especially a booming and developing economy like India. Our economy presents a diverse set of challenges, and hidden in them, opportunities for new business ventures. It endeavours to spur aspiring entrepreneurs to latch on to such opportunities and create success stories for the nation.

  • Parcipating teams will have to showcase their business plan in front of an elite judging panel who will scrutinize plan's feasibility, creativity and professionalism to help budding entrepreneurs to assess themselves. It is our aim to promote the habit of entrepreneurship.

    We expect participants to present Business Plans in the following fields:

    • 1. Agriculture: There is a lot of innovation in the mobile/IT and energy spaces that have the potential to make a huge impact on the farm.  For example, smart power systems, precision agriculture tools, farm management software, and affordable sensors are all within reach of even the smallest farmers today.

    • 2. Energy: You can generate Environment friendly solutions to take care of fragility of the earth and foster sustainable living.

    • 3. Sports: The advances from engineering material science, biomechanics, communication and information technologies has maximized the training and performance in sports. You can combine passion in sport with ideas and perhaps revolutionize the vast world of sport.

    • 4. Computer Science & Information Technology: Certain software innovations, for example business methods that are implemented in software, Web applications , Implementation of Machine Learning, Language Processing, Wireless technology.

    • 5. Others: If you have any other exciting and innovative Idea that can contribute towards India’s buzzing economy.

  • New!

    The link of the format of Executive Summary is here. The deadline to submit the executive summary is 22th March,2015. The final round of the event will be held on 28th March, 2015 at MCIIE, IIT(BHU). You should email the summaries at neeraj.dhelariya.ece12@iitbhu.ac.in

    Team

    A team can have a maximum of 5 members. Members can be from any organization. All the members may or may not be from the same organization. The event is open for teams comprising of Business school students, undergraduate students, corporate professionals, as well as other independent individuals (employed or unemployed) .

    There will be 3 rounds.

    • Round-1: Executive Summary: In this round, teams will need to submit an executive summary of their business plans.
      Note:
          a. Submission will be in a prescribed format. Entries not strictly adhering to the same shall not be considered for judging.
          b. Entries reaching us after the deadline will not be entertained in any situation.
          c. Entries will be evaluated based on the evaluation criteria mentioned in evaluation criterion.

    • Round-2: Mentor Allotment
      Teams shortlisted from round-1 will be allotted mentors. These mentors will guide the teams in their B-plan. Mentors will guide you through various aspects like financials, operational issues, marketing and HR strategy, contingency plans etc. If you have any problem regarding anything, you can discuss that with your respective mentor.

    • Round-3: Final presentation
      All the short-listed teams will be invited to IIT (BHU) campus during UDYAM’15 . Final round will comprise of presentation and Q-n-A with judges and representatives from funding agencies and incubator organizations. Top three teams will be selected as winners, first runners-up and second runners-up respectively from this round.

  • Your presentation will be judged on three basic criteria:

    • 1. CONCEPT FEASIBILITY:
      Definition of the opportunity in a clear and concise manner; Overall feasibility of the business model consisting of demand/market feasibility, financial feasibility and operational feasibility.

    • 2. NOVELTY:
      The business idea should be fresh, unique and hitherto unexplored, including sustainability of differentiation .

    • 3. DEPTH OF ANALYSIS:
      The marketing strategy, financial strategy and operational strategy should be clearly identified
