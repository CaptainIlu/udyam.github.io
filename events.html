I-Chip

  • Sponsored by:

    A VLSI event: Now there is no need to manually place gates and transistors to build electronic circuits. Describe complex circuits at an abstract level in terms of functionality and data flow by designing those circuits in HDLs. This FPGA Design Contest requires one to write the code for the given problem statement in either VHDL or Verilog and optimize it, then dump the code on the FPGA Kit to physically realize the design that was coded.

  • The floating point operations have found intensive applications in the various fields due to its great dynamic range, high precision and easy operation rules. With the increasing requirements for the floating point operations for the high-speed data signal processing and the scientific operation, the requirements for the high-speed hardware floating point arithmetic units have become more and more exigent. Although high-level languages have built-in floating-point data type, it is too complex to be synthesized automatically. Hence, an IEEE 754-compliant 32-bit floating point arithmetic unit has to be designed using Verilog code.

    • Practice Round: Participants have to design a floating point adder for performing operation of addition of two operands.
    • Final Round: Participants have to design floating point subtractor and multiplier units for performing operations of subtraction and multiplication of two operands.
    • ● There will be 2 rounds: Practice round and Final round.

    • ● The practice round will have a simple problem statement to give the participants an insight into the various challenges faced during design and debugging of solutions before the final round that involves more complex implementation.

    • ● Scoring: Points will be awarded for both Practice and Final round. The final result will be based on the total score obtained by a team in both the rounds.

    • Team Specification: A team can consist of a maximum of 2 members.

    • ● The design must cover all aspects of problem statement and shows correct simulation for all test cases.

    • ● RTL design must be synthesizable and should be targeted for FPGA technology virtex7.

    • ● Winning criterion is based on QofR (quality of result both in context of capacity and run time performance) + functionality.

    • ● Use Mentor tool Questa/Modelsim for validation. Use Mentor tool Precision to synthesize the design and generate the QofR.

Commnet

  • Does it strike your mind how do the extensive communication networks work reliably? The event is designed to be accessible to the new learners of digital communication, yet at the same time pose a challenge to the experienced students. The challenge is to implement a software model reflecting the functioning of a part of a digital communication system in the real world. The implementation should be such that the information reaches the desired device without considerable loss. The event will test your innovation potential along with the major focus on getting you familiar with the advances in communication technology.

  • Basic Level

    • ● Participants have to implement Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiplexing (OFDM) on the transmitter side in Matlab. A test sample of data will be provided to participants using which they have to make the required modulated signal with the parameters given at the time of main event.

    • ● Parameters will include sampling frequency, number of carriers, bandwidth and intermediate modulation scheme required to be used.

    Advance Level

    • ● Participants have to implement OFDM on the receiver side in Matlab on a real signal. A test signal will be provided to participants using which they have to find certain parameters like no. of carriers and modulation scheme used to make the test signal. After knowing these parameters, they have to demodulate a distorted transmitted signal into the real signal.
    • ● Quiz: A quiz will be conducted covering basic understanding of digital communication system, signal processing schemes and their implementation in Matlab.
    • ● There will be 2 levels: basic level and advance level

    • ● There will be quiz based on the concepts of digital communication and signal processing. The basic level will give the participants an insight into the implementation of transmitter side of a digital communication system in which participants can learn basic modulation and signal processing techniques.

    • ● Advance level includes the implementation of receiver side and training of a digital communication system where participants have to deal with real signals

    • ● Scoring: points will be awarded for both rounds and quiz. The final result will be based on total score obtained by a team in both the rounds and the quiz.

    • ● Software: students have to write their codes in Matlab

    • ● Team specifications: A team consists of maximum of 2 members

    • ● The implementation must satisfy all the parameters of the problem statement and shows correct output signals for all test cases

    • ● The quality of signals at the output and basic understanding of digital communication will be judging criteria for the event.

Krypto

  • Does hacking attract you? Do you want to know how your confidential information is kept secret from hackers?
    If your answer is YES! Then Cryptography is the field, answers you all these questions. Cryptography is the art and science of keeping information secure from unintended audiences. Conversely, cryptanalysis is the art and science of breaking encoded data.

    Udyam’15 presents you Krypto, an event, where you will learn how to protect your conversation safe from eavesdroppers and even how to break into secrets of others.

  • Bonus Rounds (100 points): Deadline for submission: 21th March, 2015

    • Round 1: Participants are required to build a terminal software either in java, python or C++ to encrypt/decrypt a text file(.txt) using DES CBC mode encryption without using any existing libraries of Cryptography. No extra credits for GUI.

    • Round 2: Participants are required to encrypt a file containing one/more text/image files using any authentication encryption standards. Libraries available for crypto can be used.

    Final Round (500 points):
    Participants need to break the security of RSA based cryptosystem. Public key and an encrypted message will be provided, and the message have to be retrieved

  • There will be 2 rounds: A pre-event round (bonus!) and a final on-site round.
    Pre-events to be announced soon. It will have questions on basic concepts of Cryptography and programming tasks.

    Software: Participants can use Java, C++ or Python only.

    Team specifications: A team consists of maximum of 2 members.

    There will be a special prize for the best team from first year.

  • The teams will be judged on the following criteria:

    • ● Time taken to encrypt/decrypt.
    • ● Semantic Security of the cipher.

Ignite

  • IGNITE is the business plan contest conducted as a part of UDYAM the techno-management festival of Electronics Engineering Department, IIT(BHU), Varanasi. It seeks to identify budding entrepreneurs from around the country to contribute to India’s buzzing economy.
    We believe new business ideas are the fuel for growth of any economy, especially a booming and developing economy like India. Our economy presents a diverse set of challenges, and hidden in them, opportunities for new business ventures. It endeavours to spur aspiring entrepreneurs to latch on to such opportunities and create success stories for the nation.

  • Parcipating teams will have to showcase their business plan in front of an elite judging panel who will scrutinize plan's feasibility, creativity and professionalism to help budding entrepreneurs to assess themselves. It is our aim to promote the habit of entrepreneurship.

    We expect participants to present Business Plans in the following fields:

    • 1. Agriculture: There is a lot of innovation in the mobile/IT and energy spaces that have the potential to make a huge impact on the farm.  For example, smart power systems, precision agriculture tools, farm management software, and affordable sensors are all within reach of even the smallest farmers today.

    • 2. Energy: You can generate Environment friendly solutions to take care of fragility of the earth and foster sustainable living.

    • 3. Sports: The advances from engineering material science, biomechanics, communication and information technologies has maximized the training and performance in sports. You can combine passion in sport with ideas and perhaps revolutionize the vast world of sport.

    • 4. Computer Science & Information Technology: Certain software innovations, for example business methods that are implemented in software, Web applications , Implementation of Machine Learning, Language Processing, Wireless technology.

    • 5. Others: If you have any other exciting and innovative Idea that can contribute towards India’s buzzing economy.
